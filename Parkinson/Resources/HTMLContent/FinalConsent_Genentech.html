Introductory Information and Purpose

The aim of this study is to help Roche/Genentech evaluate the feasibility of using a REDCap Cloud custom mobile app for collecting electronic patient-reported outcomes (ePRO) in clinical trials.

In order to be eligible to participate in this study, you must:

  • Be a Roche/Genentech employee. Contingent workers and interns are not eligible.
  • Be at least 18 years of age
  • Not be pregnant
  • Have an Apple iPhone
  • Reside in the Pacific Standard Timezone

The study staff may withdraw you from the study at any time, including inability to use device as instructed for the required duration.

Roche/Genentech management does not urge, influence, or encourage anyone who works for the company to take part in this study. Your participation in this research is completely voluntary. You may withdraw from the study at any time and for any reason. Your decision to not participate in the study, or a decision on your part to withdraw, will have no effect whatsoever on your employment status at Roche/Genentech. You may refuse to participate or withdraw from the study at any time without penalty or prejudice.

Roche/Genentech intends to own all the study data. With the information from the study, Roche/Genentech may obtain patents or other legal licenses relating to potential diagnostic or treatment methods. Genentech, its scientists and physicians, and its collaborators, commercial partners, affiliates or successors may obtain financial benefit. Genentech has no plans for you to share in this financial benefit.

Description of Procedures

When you agree to participate in this study, the following actions will be taken:

  • You will receive an email with a Quick Response or “QR" Code and be asked to download the REDCap Cloud app from the Apple Store.
  • You will be asked to set up an account with REDCap Cloud and answer a screening questionnaire.
  • You will be asked to review information within the app including a description of this pilot, procedures for maintaining confidentiality, equipment and questionnaires used in this pilot, and any known risks.
  • You will be asked to “sign” the informed consent within the app through an e-consent process.
  • OPTIONAL: You will be given the option of synching your step count from Apple Health Kit to the REDCap Cloud app.
  • You will be asked to complete two sets of questionnaires inquiring about your perceptions of your general quality of life and health status. NOTE: You may skip any questions you feel uncomfortable answering.
  • You will be notified via the REDCap Cloud app to complete two questionnaires every week for four (4) weeks , for a total of eight (8) questionnaires.

Confidentiality

The medical information that you share with Genentech study staff through the study questionnaire and the data collected from the REDCap Cloud app are treated as confidential medical information.

Your name will be replaced with a unique numeric participant ID code to ensure your identity is kept private and the data you submit is not linked to you. The coded data will be encrypted and stored on a secure REDCap Cloud server. The risk of loss of confidentiality of your data is very low. Only study staff will have access to the key that links your name to your unique participant ID number.

Although the research in this study will be done in a manner that does not identify you, it may be necessary to link the participant code assigned to you back to your data. This process is called “re-identification.” Your data will only be “re-identified” in order to contact you if an investigator would like to collect additional data from you, if the research results need to be part of the information sent to the FDA or other regulatory agency or if there is discovery of a potential medical issue that would require you to follow up with your personal physician. “Re-identification” will be done in a manner that maintains participant confidentiality.

You will have access to copies of all the data you submit.

EXPERIMENTAL SUBJECT’S BILL OF RIGHTS

California law, under Health and Safety Code Section 24172, requires that anyone who is asked to be in a research study (medical experiment), or who is asked to agree for someone else to be in a research study, has the right to know the following:

  1. Both the nature and reason for the research study or experiment.
  2. What will happen during the research study, and what drug or device will be used.
  3. Any expected discomforts and risks from taking part in the research.
  4. Any possible benefits to being in the research.
  5. The risks and benefits of any non-experimental medical procedures, drugs, or devices that could be used instead of being in the research.
  6. How research related complications will be treated during and after the research study is over.
  7. Questions can be asked about the research and about anything that will be done during the research study.
  8. The research subject can stop taking part in the research study at any time for any reason without penalty or loss of benefits.
  9. A copy of their signed and dated consent form will be given to subjects.
  10. The research subject can take the time needed to decide if they want to take part in the research study. No one can pressure, force or unduly influence any person to take part in research.
